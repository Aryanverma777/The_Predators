Government Agricultural Schemes

Explore various government initiatives designed to support farmers

Pradhan Mantri Kisan Samman Nidhi (PM-KISAN)

Financial

Direct income support to farmers' families.

Eligibility

Small and marginal farmers with cultivable landholding

Benefits

₹6,000 per year in three equal installments

Apply Now

Kisan Credit Card

Financial

Provides farmers with affordable credit for their agricultural needs.

Eligibility

All farmers, sharecroppers, and tenant farmers

Benefits

Interest subvention of 2% for loans up to ₹3 lakhs, additional 3% for timely repayment

Apply Now

Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana

Insurance

Crop insurance scheme to protect farmers from crop failure.

Eligibility

All farmers growing notified crops

Benefits

Insurance coverage and financial support in case of crop failure due to natural calamities

Apply Now

Weather Based Crop Insurance Scheme

Insurance

Insurance against adverse weather conditions.

Eligibility

Farmers in notified areas growing notified crops

Benefits

Compensation for financial losses due to adverse weather conditions

Apply Now

Sub-Mission on Agricultural Mechanization

Technology

Promotes farm mechanization among small and marginal farmers.

Eligibility

Individual farmers, custom hiring centers, farm machinery banks

Benefits

Financial assistance for purchasing agricultural machinery and equipment

Apply Now

National Mission for Sustainable Agriculture

Technology

Promotes sustainable agriculture practices.

Eligibility

All farmers

Benefits

Support for adopting climate-resilient technologies and practices

Apply Now

How to Apply for Schemes

Follow these steps to apply for government agricultural schemes

1

Check Eligibility

Review the eligibility criteria for the scheme you're interested in.

2

Prepare Documents

Gather all required documents such as land records, identity proof, and bank details.

3

Submit Application

Apply online through the official portal or visit your local agriculture office for assistance.

4

Track Application

Monitor your application status through the portal or by contacting the agriculture office.
